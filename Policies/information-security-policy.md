# Information Security Policy

**TechNova Solutions**  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** April 2026  
**Owner:** Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)

## 1. Purpose
This policy establishes the framework for protecting TechNova's information assets, ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA triad) while supporting business objectives and regulatory compliance (e.g., SOC 2, NIST CSF 2.0).

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, vendors, and systems handling TechNova information, including cloud infrastructure, customer data, and internal networks.

## 3. Roles and Responsibilities
- **CISO**: Overall accountability for the Information Security Management System (ISMS).
- **Managers**: Ensure team compliance and risk reporting.
- **Employees/Contractors**: Follow policies, report incidents, and complete training.
- **IT/Security Team**: Implement technical controls and monitor compliance.

## 4. Policy Statements
- Information assets must be classified and protected according to sensitivity.
- Risk management processes will identify, assess, and treat risks.
- Access is granted on a need-to-know and least-privilege basis.
- All users must complete annual security awareness training.
- Incidents must be reported within 1 hour of discovery.
- Third-party vendors undergo risk assessment before engagement.
- Compliance with applicable laws (GDPR considerations, CCPA if relevant) and standards (NIST, ISO 27001) is mandatory.

## 5. Enforcement
Violations may result in disciplinary action, up to termination and legal action. Audits will be conducted periodically.

## 6. References
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 (Govern function)
- ISO 27001:2022 Annex A controls
- SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security)

## 7. Review and Approval
Reviewed annually or after significant changes. Approved by: CISO and Executive Leadership.
