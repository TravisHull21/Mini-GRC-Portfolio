# Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

**TechNova Solutions**  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** April 2026

## 1. Purpose
To define acceptable and unacceptable use of TechNova's information systems, networks, and devices to protect company assets and reduce risk.

## 2. Scope
Applies to all users (employees, contractors, etc.) accessing TechNova resources, including laptops, cloud services, email, and internet.

## 3. Acceptable Use
- Use systems primarily for business purposes.
- Personal use is allowed if minimal, does not interfere with work, and complies with this policy.
- Protect credentials; use MFA where required.
- Report suspicious activity immediately.

## 4. Prohibited Activities (Unacceptable Use)
- Unauthorized access or sharing of sensitive data.
- Installing unapproved software or bypassing security controls.
- Downloading or distributing copyrighted material illegally.
- Engaging in illegal, harassing, or discriminatory activities.
- Using company resources for personal financial gain or competing businesses.
- Introducing malware or attempting to disable security features.
- Sharing passwords or allowing account sharing.
- Excessive personal use that impacts performance or bandwidth.

## 5. Monitoring and Privacy
TechNova reserves the right to monitor all activity on its systems. Users should have no expectation of complete privacy.

## 6. Enforcement
Violations will result in disciplinary action, including revocation of access, termination, and potential legal consequences.

## 7. Acknowledgment
All users must acknowledge this policy upon hiring and annually.

**References**: NIST PR.AT (Awareness and Training), SOC 2 Common Criteria.
